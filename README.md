# Australian Bushfire Analysis Project 🔥🌲

## Overview

This project examines and compares two of the most catastrophic bushfire seasons in Australian history: the 2008-2009 Black Saturday fires and 2019-2020 Black Summer bushfires. The first dataset captures the period where the Black Saturday fires devastated large portions of Queensland and Victoria. The second dataset encompasses the Black Summer fires, which burned over 18 million hectares nationwide and caused severe ecological and environmental damage.

## Tools

* QGIS
* NASA FIRMS datasets (.csv) for Australia
* ESRI Satellite Raster Imagery

## Key Steps

* Imported bushfire event data into QGIS
* Mapped fire locations across Australia
* Generated spatial density visualizations
* Created map layouts to highlight regional patterns

## Result

The comparison shows that bushfire activity in 2019-2020 was significantly more widespread and intense than in 2008-2009. Areas with substantial vegetation—whether coastal, inland or remote—consistently produced the highest fire concentrations, emphasising the role of fuel load over simple geography location. The timing of both datasets during the summer months further contributed to increased fire activity due to hotter, drier conditions. Overall, the maps demonstrate how vegetation distribution and seasonal climate combine to shape Australia’s bushfire patterns at a national scale.
